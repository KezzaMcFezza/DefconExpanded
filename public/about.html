Welcome to DEFCON Expanded

Welcome to the Official Website for DEFCON Expanded, the 8-16 player mod for DEFCON. The mod is months in the making and wouldn't have been possible without some talented people who are passionate about the game. The mod is still in its infancy and we suggest you email me or contact one of us on Discord if any bugs or issues arise.

The website is also currently still in development, so dont be concerned if you see any issues or broken elements. I am currently making live changes so things might change quickly from one day to another, i hope everyone understands

New Gameplay Features

Bomber Launch (Empty)

The new empty bomber launch mode allows you to launch a bomber without arming a nuke, allowing you to use the bomber to pick up a warhead at the base of your choosing. This provides more strategic flexibility in managing your nuclear arsenal.

Color Picker

On opening the Alliance toolbar tab, you will see a new window that lists all of the team colors. You can now choose your team color, adding a new level of customization to your gameplay experience.

New Graphical Features

16 Available Team Colors!

We've expanded the color palette from the original 6 colors to 16, giving players more options to distinguish their teams. The new colors include Green, Red, Blue, Yellow, Cyan, Pink, Black, Orange, Olive, Scarlet, Indigo, Gold, Dark Teal, Purple, White, and Brown.

Unit Outlines

To improve visibility, especially for darker colors and units over the ocean, we've added a thin white highlight around every unit.

The original DEFCON was split into 6 continent-like territories. With DEFCON Expanded, we are able to introduce more players and thus the need to divide the world into more parts. 8 Players attempt to keep as true to the vanilla experience as possible, but with 16 player it is simply not possible to adhere to purely geographic boundaries any more.

So, we have decided to divide the world along not just geographic lines but also regional cultural ones to make sensible factions which could plausibly present a united front while being balanced in territorial size relative to each other. Another factor is the number of cities in each territory. In default mode, each territory should have a minimum of 25 cities, but some parts of the world are so sparsely populated that no amount of pure territorial coverage would give them the required city count.

Starting with the Americas, North America has been split between the USA and Canada. Canada has a lot of empty space and no cities, so I decided to combine it with parts of Europe, that will be touched on later. South America is split between Hispanic Latin America and Brazil, with Hispanic America again split into a cleaner north and south.

In Europe, to Canada goes the British Isles, but due to lack of cities, I also added Scandinavia.

Europe has been much reduced in size, but is still comparable to the other factions.

Since I don't believe in splitting apart individual countries, Russia remains intact as its own faction.

Africa is split into three parts - West/Central, South/East, and North. The division between central and south follows the borders of Congo, while the division between north and center follows the borders of the Afro-Asiatic countries (so technically not Arabic but the larger family that Arabic is a part of, which includes Ethiopia).

Asia is split between China, India, and the islands.

In between Eurasia is the Middle East which is the collection of non-Arabic, non-Indic countries between Europe, Russia, and China. Thankfully it happens to be roughly analogous to other factions in size.

And Finally we have Australia and Antarctica to round out the extreme edge of the planet's territory. Some of Antarctica is part of Australia in order to give Australia the appropriate number of cities, though this does mean Antarctic territory is left over as the last territory with significantly fewer cities than 25, unless we add more city data later.

Note that in order to have any population at all in those southernmost cities, we used data on penguin colonies rather than human settlements. It seems this game takes place in an alternate universe where sentient Penguins have armed themselves with nuclear weapons.
